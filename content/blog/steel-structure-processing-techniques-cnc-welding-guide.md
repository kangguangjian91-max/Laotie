# Steel Structure Processing: CNC Cutting, Welding & Surface Treatment Guide

Steel structure processing is where engineering meets craftsmanship. Raw steel plates and sections are transformed into precision building components through a sequence of controlled industrial processes. Understanding these techniques helps buyers evaluate supplier capability, compare quality, and specify the right processing requirements for their projects.

## CNC Cutting: The Foundation of Precision Fabrication

Modern steel fabrication begins with computer-controlled cutting. Manual cutting is virtually extinct in professional factories — CNC (Computer Numerical Control) technology ensures every component matches the shop drawing within millimeter tolerances.

### Plasma Cutting

CNC plasma cutting is the workhorse of steel structure processing. It uses a high-velocity jet of ionized gas to melt and remove metal:

- **Cutting thickness**: 3mm to 50mm steel plate (up to 80mm for heavy-duty machines)
- **Accuracy**: ±0.5mm for plates up to 25mm thickness
- **Speed**: 500-3,500 mm/min depending on material thickness
- **Best for**: Regular structural plates, gusset plates, base plates, stiffeners

### Laser Cutting

For complex profiles, tight tolerances, or thinner materials, fiber laser cutting delivers superior results:

- **Cutting thickness**: 0.5mm to 25mm (fiber laser)
- **Accuracy**: ±0.1mm — unmatched by any thermal cutting method
- **Edge quality**: Minimal heat-affected zone, near-machined finish
- **Best for**: Connection plates with tight bolt patterns, architectural details, complex geometries

### Flame Cutting (Oxy-Fuel)

For heavy steel plates beyond plasma capability:

- **Cutting thickness**: 25mm to 300mm
- **Accuracy**: ±1.5mm
- **Best for**: Column base plates, thick flange plates, crane runway beams

### Quality Control at Cutting Stage

Every cut piece is inspected against the shop drawing before moving to assembly:

| Check | Tolerance | Method |
|-------|-----------|--------|
| Overall dimensions | ±1mm (critical), ±2mm (general) | Steel tape + caliper |
| Edge squareness | 90° ± 0.5° | Try square |
| Hole position | ±0.5mm | Go/no-go gauge |
| Surface roughness | Ra ≤ 25μm | Visual comparator |

> **Key insight for buyers**: Ask your supplier what cutting tolerance they guarantee. Precision cutting reduces assembly issues and on-site rework by up to 40%.

## Welding Methods: The Backbone of Structural Integrity

Welding quality determines whether a steel structure stands for 5 years or 50. Different joint types require different welding processes — a professional factory deploys multiple methods.

### Submerged Arc Welding (SAW)

The preferred method for long, straight seams in H-beams and box sections:

- **Process**: Arc burns beneath a blanket of granular flux — no visible arc, no spatter
- **Penetration**: Deep, consistent fusion into the root of the joint
- **Productivity**: 3-5× faster than manual welding for beam fabrication
- **Applications**: Web-to-flange seams on H-columns and H-rafters, plate girder fabrication

At OldTie, all primary structural members are welded using automatic SAW machines with real-time parameter monitoring.

### CO₂ Gas Shielded Welding (GMAW)

The versatile choice for connections, stiffeners, and secondary members:

- **Process**: Continuous wire electrode with CO₂ or Ar/CO₂ shielding gas
- **Advantage**: All-position capability — works for horizontal, vertical, and overhead joints
- **Applications**: Column-to-base-plate connections, stiffener plates, purlin cleats, bracing connections

### Manual Arc Welding (SMAW)

Used selectively for complex joints where automation is impractical:

- **Applications**: Field weld preparations, temporary assembly, repair welding
- **Limitation**: Lower productivity, higher operator skill dependency

### Welding Quality Control

Structural welding isn't complete without non-destructive testing (NDT):

| Test Method | What It Detects | Coverage Rate |
|-------------|-----------------|---------------|
| Visual Testing (VT) | Surface defects, weld profile, spatter | 100% of all welds |
| Ultrasonic Testing (UT) | Internal porosity, lack of fusion, cracks | 10-20% of critical welds |
| Magnetic Particle Testing (MT) | Surface and near-surface cracks | Supplementary to UT |
| Radiographic Testing (RT) | Internal defects (critical joints) | Full-penetration butt welds |

**Applicable standards**: AWS D1.1 (USA), EN 1090-2 (Europe), GB 50661 (China). OldTie holds EN 1090-2 EXC2 certification — verifying our welding procedures meet European structural requirements.

## Straightening: Correcting Thermal Distortion

Welding introduces heat, and heat causes distortion. Every welded steel member passes through a straightening process before surface treatment:

- **H-beam straightening machine**: Hydraulic rollers apply controlled pressure to correct flange tilt, web waviness, and camber deviation
- **Tolerance**: Straightness deviation ≤ L/1000 (1mm per meter of length)
- **Flame straightening**: Localized heating for complex distortions (used sparingly, by certified operators)

> **Why it matters**: A column that's 10mm out of straight at the factory becomes 50mm out at the building eave — enough to cause alignment failures during erection.

## Surface Treatment: Corrosion Protection That Lasts

Surface preparation and coating are the final — and arguably most important — processing step. Poor surface treatment is the #1 cause of premature steel structure degradation.

### Shot Blasting (Surface Preparation)

Before any paint touches steel, the surface must be prepared:

- **Process**: Steel shot or grit is propelled at high velocity to remove mill scale, rust, and welding slag
- **Standard**: SA 2.5 (near-white metal) per ISO 8501-1 — the industry benchmark for structural steel
- **Result**: A clean, profiled surface with 50-75μm roughness for optimal paint adhesion

### Painting Systems

| System | Coats | Total DFT | Best For | Lifespan |
|--------|-------|-----------|----------|----------|
| Alkyd (standard) | Primer + topcoat | 80-120μm | Indoor, mild climate | 5-10 years |
| Epoxy (intermediate) | Zinc-rich primer + epoxy MIO + PU topcoat | 200-280μm | Outdoor, industrial | 15-25 years |
| Polysiloxane (premium) | Zinc primer + polysiloxane | 200-250μm | Coastal, chemical exposure | 20-30 years |

### Hot-Dip Galvanizing (HDG)

For maximum corrosion protection in aggressive environments:

- **Process**: Steel components are immersed in molten zinc at ~450°C
- **Coating thickness**: 85-200μm depending on steel thickness (per ISO 1461)
- **Lifespan**: 30-50+ years in most environments
- **Best for**: Coastal projects, wastewater treatment plants, agricultural buildings, transmission towers

## Drilling and Pre-Assembly

### CNC Drilling

Connection holes are drilled after surface treatment to avoid paint filling:

- **Accuracy**: ±0.5mm hole position, H12 tolerance on hole diameter
- **Standard**: Holes are 2mm oversized relative to bolt diameter (per AISC/EN)
- **Marking**: Each component is stamped with its unique erection drawing reference number

### Factory Pre-Assembly

For complex or large-span structures, a partial or full pre-assembly test is conducted:

- Random bays are assembled in the factory to verify connection fit
- Any misalignment is corrected before shipping
- Pre-assembly reduces on-site installation issues by up to 70%
